Single variable calculus has significant applications in many fields including physics, engineering, economics, and biology. It provides a framework for understanding and modeling change, and is essential for solving problems in a variety of fields.

# Functions

Functions are mathematical objects that take inputs (also known as arguments or variables) and produce outputs (also known as values or results). Polynomials, trigonometric functions, and power laws are all types of functions.

# Limits

Limits describe the behavior of a function as the input approaches a particular value. They are used to define the behavior of functions at points where they may not be defined, and to understand the continuity of functions.

# Continuity
Continuity is a property of functions that states that the function is well-behaved and has no abrupt changes or discontinuities. This concept is important for understanding the behavior of functions and for defining derivatives and integrals.

# Differentiation

Differentiation is the process of finding the rate of change of a function at a particular point. The power, product, and chain rules are techniques used to differentiate functions composed of multiple terms.

# Integration
Integration is the reverse process of differentiation. It is used to find the total amount or cumulative effect of a changing quantity. The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus connects differentiation and integration by stating that differentiation and integration are inverse operations.
