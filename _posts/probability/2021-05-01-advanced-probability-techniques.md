# Geometric Probability
Geometric Probability: Geometric probability deals with the probability of a random process taking a certain number of steps before achieving a certain outcome. It is used to model problems where the outcome depends on a sequence of independent and identically distributed events.

# Bijections
Bijections: A bijection is a mathematical function that maps one set of elements to another set in a one-to-one and onto manner. Bijections are used in probability theory to establish a correspondence between sets and to facilitate the calculation of probabilities.

# Recursion
Recursion: Recursion is a technique in which a function is defined in terms of itself. Recursion is often used in probability theory to model problems where the outcome at a given step depends on the outcome of the previous step.

# Markov Chains
Markov Chains: A Markov chain is a mathematical model for a system that transitions from one state to another over time. Markov chains are used to model problems where the outcome at a given step depends only on the outcome of the previous step, and not on any other steps.

# Generating Functions
Generating Functions: A generating function is a mathematical object that encodes the distribution of a random variable. Generating functions are used to calculate the probabilities of certain events and to study the behavior of random variables.
