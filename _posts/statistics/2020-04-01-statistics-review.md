# Measures of Central Tendency
The measures of central tendency, such as mean, median, and mode, provide a summary of the data and describe where the "center" of the data is located. The interpretation of these measures depends on the shape of the data and the presence of outliers.

* Median
* Mode

# Measures of Dispersion
The measures of dispersion, such as range, variance, and standard deviation, provide information about the spread of the data and describe how much the data deviates from the center. A large dispersion indicates that the data is spread out, while a small dispersion indicates that the data is clustered.

* Range
* Variance
* Standard Deviation

## Hypothesis Testing

In hypothesis testing, the null hypothesis is a statement about the population that is being tested, and the alternative hypothesis is the statement that the researcher wants to prove. The p-value is calculated based on the sample data and the null hypothesis, and it represents the probability of observing a result as extreme or more extreme than the one observed if the null hypothesis is true.

If the p-value is small (typically less than 0.05), it means that the likelihood of observing a result as extreme or more extreme than the one observed if the null hypothesis is true is small, and the null hypothesis can be rejected. This means that the results are statistically significant and support the alternative hypothesis.

On the other hand, if the p-value is large (typically greater than 0.05), it means that the likelihood of observing a result as extreme or more extreme than the one observed if the null hypothesis is true is large, and the null hypothesis cannot be rejected. This means that the results are not statistically significant and do not support the alternative hypothesis.

The p-value is an important concept in hypothesis testing and provides a measure of the evidence against the null hypothesis. However, it is important to note that a small p-value does not necessarily mean that the results are causally significant or that the alternative hypothesis is true.
